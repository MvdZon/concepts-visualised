The size of the Russian economy

A closer look at the GDP(gross domestic product) of Russia relative to other economies, for a better understanding of Russia's relative economic power

The eleven largest economies in the world

Russia's GDP is $1,862 billion, this makes it only the 11th largest economy in the world

It is much smaller than both The USA($26,950 billion) and China($17,701 billion)

The Russian economy compared to the rest of Europe

In Europe, Russia is the 5th largest economy. It is only 40% the size of the German economy($4,430 billion) and it's smaller than Italy($2,186 billion)

The Russian economy compared to small countries

The Russian economy is equal in size to South Korea($1,709 billion) or the combined economies of Belgium + The Netherlands($1,720 billion)

South Korea is 171x smaller in land size

The Netherlands is 412x smaller in land size

Belgium is 560x smaller in land size

The Russian economy compared to the United States

The USA has a GDP of $26,950 billion while Russia has a GDP of $1,862 billion. This means it is 13 times smaller

What does 13 times smaller mean?

The average height of a man, worldwide, is 1.70m(5.5 feet). 13 times smaller is 13cm(0.5 feet)

A regular computer mouse is about 14-15cm. Your computer mouse is relatively larger to you than Russia is to The USA

The Russian economy compared to Ethiopia

Ethiopia has a GDP of $156 billion. Which is roughly 13 times smaller than Russia's gdp

Ethiopia has the same relative economic power to Russia as Russia does to The United States

The Russian economy compared to Puerto Rico

Puerto Rico has a GDP of 118 billion which makes it 15 times smaller than Russia

Russia's GDP is 13 times smaller than that of The USA

Puerto Rico's relative power to Russia is almost equal to Russia's power compared to The USA
